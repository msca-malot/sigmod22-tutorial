# S  A  K  E 

As knowledge graphs (KGs) are increasingly acquiring spatial aspects, natural language spatial question answering (NLSQA) in KG is pertinent for location-based applications. The major challenges for NLSQA include data heterogeneity due to spatial and non-spatial aspects in KG, recognition of spatial constraints from questions, and integration of spatial closeness and semantic relevance in answers. In this project, we propose a framework for Spatial question Answering over Knowledge graph based on Embedding techniques (SAKE).

Natural Language Spatial Question (NLSQ)

An NLSQ can be answered by a list of spatial facts. Each of them interprets the answer by matching the NLSQ's mentioned relation and class and being close to the NLSQ's mentioned spatial range if applicable. Non-exhaustively, we study four typical NLSQs that are identified based on how the entity, entity class, and spatial range of interests are uttered as follows.

  • A basic NLSQ asks for some entities having the mentioned relation with the mentioned entity.
  • A class-based NLSQ asks for some mentioned class of entities having the mentioned relation with the mentioned entity or mentioned class of entities.
  • A nearby NLSQ extends a class-based NLSQ such that the asked entities should also be close to the user's location.
  • A location-name NLSQ extends a class-based NLSQ such that the asked entities should also be close to the location whose name is mentioned by the question.
